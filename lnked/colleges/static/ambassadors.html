What's your school really like?

Student Ambassadors are here to answer your questions and let you know what life at your prospective university is really like - no cheezy tours, no sugar coating.


Nebraska Lincoln

"There really is no place like Nebraska. As an out of state student, I was terrified to come to such a large school knowing close to nobody. However I was able to get involved in so many clubs and organizations that allowed me to meet so many great people. The resources we have through the library and your own college are absolutely amazing, and you'll never feel lost because there's always someone there to help."

- Henry Clarke

Southeast Community College

"Between the affordable tuition and fees, the small class sizes, the convenience of the campus location and online, or the friendly and courteous faculty and staff, it's hard not to have a positive experience at SCC. I still get to live in downtown Lincoln and visit with all my friends in the area, but for a fraction of the cost!"

- Rose Anderson


Nebraska Wesleyan

"Nebraska Wesleyan is rated the #1 liberal arts university in Nebraska, and that doesn't surprise me at all. The small class sizes allow you to grow closer to your professors and fellow students, most of which share your similar passions. If it weren't for the amazing professors I've met along the years I wouldn't feel as prepared to graduate as I am now!"

- Brian Herman

Union College

"I think Union is a great place to be. Teachers are not afraid to go the extra mile to help you with a homework assignment, you make friends who have your back - spiritually and academically, and the small campus provides endless service and leadership experiences!"

- Mary Collins
